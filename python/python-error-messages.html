Reading error messages

Error messages are meant to be as easy as possible to understand. But there comes a problem: they were created by really good developers who thought that their error messages were easy to understand but really their not unless you know what to look for or you're a brilliant programmer that uses computers every free second of their lives. Which isn't most normal humans.

Where you've gone wrong

Most of the time, errors come about because of a typo so knowing where you've gone wrong is REALLY useful.

In every error message, there will be the words traceback (most recent call last): and then the file you're creating (or <pyshell#0> if you're working in the shell)
